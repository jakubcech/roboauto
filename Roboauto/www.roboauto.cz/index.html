Our Expertise
Sensor data fusion
We develop methods to integrate multiple sensor data sources. This creates more consistent, accurate and useful world models that improve self-driving capabilities.
Simulations
One of the most important parts of developing self-driving cars is simulation. Simulations reduce the cost of testing in the real world. We currently use simulations in both Gazebo and GTA V. This allows us to develop new, safer algorithms much faster.
Validations
We cooperate with sensor suppliers to validate sensor performance and measure KPIs for particular testing scenarios.
Path planning
We are able to model the real world in a way that produces reliable and safe path plans. Path plans that ensure the car reaches its destination.

Technology
Object detection
We use YOLO and Full Resolution Residual Networks (FRRN) segmentation to correctly recognize the type of tracked objects.
ROS
Robot Operating System is the fundamental framework of our solutions.
Cameras, Radars and LiDARs
We use standard sensors to detect roads and objects near vehicles. We are also able to employ other available sensors, like GPS, IMU, odometry, car CAN bus information, and more.
Lane detection
We use deep neural networks and probabilistic algorithms for lane detection.
AI - Neural networks
We are using deep neural networks widely to improve self-driving capabilities of Roboauto. Every single drive and simulation improves our models and makes the car more robust against any situation it may encounter on the road.
Probabilistic robotics
Probabilistic robotics plays a vital role in our algorithms. We use Bayesian filters (for example, particle filters, Kalman filter), probabilistic approach (Markov chains, MCMC, Gibbs, Metropolis-Hastings) and its improvements for simultaneous localization and mapping (SLAM). Or for object-based modeling of the surrounding environment and other problems in this field.

About Roboauto
Roboauto is a self-driving startup located in Brno, Czech Republic.
Our team formed in 2007, started on smaller car models and built its first full-scale car prototype in 2014. The team has 15 software developers and technicians.
